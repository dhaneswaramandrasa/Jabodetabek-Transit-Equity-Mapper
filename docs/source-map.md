## Synthesis (Preliminary)

### What the literature establishes
- Transit equity measurement using Gini coefficients and spatial analysis is well-established (Delmelle & Casas 2012)
- Jakarta-specific transit research exists but focuses on BRT accessibility and TOD potential
- Developing-country transit equity studies are growing (Cali, Accra, Belo Horizonte)

### The gap your research fills
- **No composite need-vs-access framework for full Jabodetabek** (not just DKI Jakarta)
- **No dual-resolution comparison** (kelurahan vs H3) exposing MAUP in equity scoring
- **No what-if scenario simulation embedded in equity framework**
- **No three-way cost model** (transit vs car vs motorcycle) for mode choice equity
